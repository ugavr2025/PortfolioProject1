Assignment Overview

This assignment is the first of two major portfolio projects for this course. The goal of this portfolio is to demonstrate your understanding and application of core concepts in virtual reality development. You will create four distinct demonstrations, each focusing on different fundamental aspects of VR creation: 3D transformations and lighting, physics simulation, VR interaction, and advanced object manipulation.

This is an individual project. While you may discuss concepts with your peers, the implementation and final submission must be your own work.

Demo 1: Solar System Simulation

Objective: To demonstrate foundational skills in 3D transformations, object hierarchies, and lighting by creating a basic model of the Sun, Earth, and the Moon.

This section outlines the core requirements for building your solar system model. Focus on the accuracy of the motion and lighting principles.

Core Requirements:

  • Scene Objects: Your scene must contain at least three primary objects representing the Sun, the Earth, and the Moon. Use various Materials to create better visuals (colors or Texture Maps).
  • Orbital Mechanics: The Earth must orbit the Sun, and the Moon must orbit the Earth. Prioritize accurate relative orbital periods and distances (scaled for viewability).
  • Earth's Rotation and Tilt: The Earth must rotate on its own axis, tilted at approximately 23.5 degrees to demonstrate the cause of seasons.
  • Lighting: The Sun must be the primary light source, dynamically illuminating the Earth and Moon to create correct day/night cycles and moon phases.

Demo 2: Rube Goldberg Physics Machine

Objective: To demonstrate proficiency with the physics engine, including rigid bodies, colliders, joints, and event-driven actions.

This demo is all about cause and effect. Build a chain reaction that is entirely driven by Unity's physics system.

Core Requirements:

  • Chain Reaction: Create a sequence of at least five distinct, connected physical events.
  • Physics-Driven: The entire chain reaction must be driven by the physics engine, not manual animation.
  • Collision and Events: Connections between events must be based on physics interactions (collisions, triggers).
  • The "Trap": The final event must be a clear action, like a cage falling or a platform dropping.
  • Custom Model: At least one of the objects in your simulation must be something you personally modeled in Blender (or a similar program).

Demo 3: Basic VR Exergame

Objective: To create a simple, functional rhythm-based exercise game that utilizes tracked VR controllers, demonstrating an understanding of player movement and interaction in a VR space.

Build a game that gets the player moving. The focus is on translating physical hand movements into successful game interactions.

Core Requirements:

  • VR Headset and Controllers: The game must run on a VR headset and use two tracked hand controllers.
  • Gameplay Loop: Objects must be generated and move towards the player, who must physically hit them with their controllers.
  • Interaction and Feedback: Provide clear visual or audio feedback for successful hits. Implement a scoring system.
  • User Interface (UI): A simple, in-world UI element must display the player's current score.

Demo 4: Interactive Puzzle Game

Objective: To design and build an interactive puzzle that requires the player to use advanced object manipulation skills‚Äîsuch as selecting, grabbing, releasing, and throwing‚Äîto reach a solution.

This is your chance to be creative. Design a puzzle where the solution depends on direct, nuanced object manipulation in VR.

Core Requirements:

  • Advanced Manipulation: Implement selecting/highlighting, intuitive grabbing/releasing, and realistic throwing of objects.
  • Puzzle Design: Create a puzzle with a clear goal and solution that depends on the direct manipulation of objects (e.g., placing gears, arranging blocks, using a key).
  • State Management: The game must track the puzzle's state and recognize when it has been solved.
  • Feedback and Completion: Provide clear audio-visual feedback when the player successfully solves the puzzle.
